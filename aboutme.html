About Me

Lev Estevez is a tenor trombone player about to graduate from LaGuardia High School. She started playing at the age of 7 and has been in countless music programs, a few recent ones being Juilliard Pre-College, the National Youth Orchestra of Los Angeles under the direction of Gustavo Dudamel, and French Woods Festival of the Performing Arts. She will attend Bard College for trombone performance and another undeclared major starting fall 2025 where she will continue her studies with Mr. Demian Austin of the Metropolitan Opera Orchestra. Lev hopes to become a professional orchestral musician as she has been inspired by so many talented musicians in the industry.

Lev enjoys musicals and has played in the pits for many of them at LaGuardia and French Woods. At school, she participated in the musical pit of "The Addams Family," as well as the operetta pits for Gilbert and Sullivan's "The Gondoliers" and Offenbach's "La Belle Helene." At French Woods, she participated in many more like "Ragtime," "Oklahoma," "The Hunchback of Notre Dame", and "The Music Man" to name a few. She has also been a member of LaGuardia's Philharmonic Orchestra and Wind Symphony for three years. One of her favorite symphonies is Tchaikovsky's Fifth.
